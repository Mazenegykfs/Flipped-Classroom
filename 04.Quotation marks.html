Using Quotation Marks in Technical Writing

1. Objectives

  • Use quotation marks to enclose direct quotations correctly.
  • Place other punctuation (commas, periods, etc.) correctly in relation to quotation marks.
  • Use quotation marks for titles of articles, chapters, and other short works.
  • Recognize when quotation marks are used to indicate technical terms or words used in a special sense.

2. Review

Quotation marks (" ") are used to set off the exact words of a speaker or writer. They are also used for the titles of short works and to highlight specific terms.

3. Warm-up

What is the difference in meaning between these two sentences?

1. The manager said the system is offline.

2. The manager said, "The system is offline."

Sentence 1 is an indirect quote (paraphrasing), while Sentence 2 is a direct quote, indicating those were the manager's exact words.

4. Presentation: Key Quotation Mark Rules

Rule 1: Direct Quotations

Use quotation marks to enclose the exact words of a person.

Example: The lead engineer stated, "All systems are nominal."

Rule 2: Punctuation Placement

Commas and periods always go inside the closing quotation mark.

Example: "The test is complete," she announced.

Colons and semicolons go outside the closing quotation mark.

Example: He called the results "a major breakthrough"; the team celebrated afterward.

Rule 3: Titles of Short Works

Use quotation marks for titles of articles, chapters, essays, and reports.

Example: Please read the chapter titled "Safety Procedures."
